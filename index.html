02 — INFRASTRUCTURE

The reward layer, specified

This is the engine underneath pillars 01, 02, and 04 — not a separate product.

Layer Choice Why
Network Base (primary)
Arbitrum / Optimism (backup)		 Sub-cent transaction fees; Base's Coinbase integration gives older users a familiar off-ramp
Onboarding Privy / Dynamic embedded wallets Google or Apple login creates the wallet in the background — no seed phrase for the 40+ segment
Payout unit USDC by default,
ETH on conversion		 Predictable value for both budgeting and user trust; removes the mismatch between task effort and reward value
Accrual Off-chain points balance Zero gas cost until the member actually claims
Claim Threshold batch claim, $5–10 Minimizes on-chain transaction count and keeps payouts feeling tangible
Task platform Layer3, Galxe, or TaskOn Automates quest verification and payout so nothing is sent by hand

Why not price rewards in ETH directly — ETH has moved more than 50% within single-year windows before. A survey payout priced in ETH can lose real value between completion and claim. Pricing in USDC and offering ETH as an optional conversion keeps the reward's value stable while still letting members hold crypto if they want to.

03 — RISK

Guardrails before launch

None of these are reasons to abandon the model — they're the difference between a pilot and a liability.

Sybil & bot farming

Ad-for-crypto is one of the most farmed reward models that exists. Without proof-of-personhood or behavioral fraud detection, a meaningful share of the pool goes to fake accounts, and sponsors stop paying for fraudulent engagement.

Regulatory exposure

Cross-border crypto payouts can trigger money transmission licensing, KYC/AML thresholds, and consumer protection scrutiny — reviewed jurisdiction by jurisdiction, before launch, not after.

Tax & custody

Claimed rewards are typically taxable income the moment they land. Confirm whether embedded wallets are self-custodial or platform-custodial — it changes who's liable if something goes wrong.
